Fumulus Clouds

These are a variety of pyrocumulus clouds that form above industrial cooling towers. The cooling towers release already condensed moisture into the air which rises to form clouds, some can persist for a very long time and grow into larger long-lasting formations. I do not have any photos of fumulus clouds in my collection, however I do have a photo of some cooling towers that we passed one day. The Cloud Appreciation Society released a book called ‘A Pig with Six Legs and Other Clouds’ edited by Gavin Pretor-Pinney and which features a fumulus cloud on the front cover of what looks like a pig with six legs.
