Nacreous Clouds

These clouds can be known as ‘Nacreous’, ‘Mother of Pearl’ or ‘Polar Stratospheric’ clouds and appear very high in the atmosphere; 15-30km, 10-20 miles above the Earth. They can be seen best in the northern hemisphere at latitudes higher than 50°.

They form in the freezing temperatures of the lower stratosphere (layer above the troposphere where most clouds form) at around -80°C (-112°F). Usually they are a mixture of nitric acid and ice crystals that come from parcels of moist air that get pushed through the troposphere and the boundary, tropopause, by orographic oscillations that are also responsible for high lenticular cloud formation.

You would be most likely to be see them during winter sunrises and sunsets when the sky is dark but the sun lights the clouds up and they shimmer in pastel colours. The colour display is because of the height of the ice crystals and the angles that they are arranged in to reflect and refract the sunlight.
