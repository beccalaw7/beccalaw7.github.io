Hello, my name is Becca and I am a student from Cambridge with a keen interest in clouds and the weather. I have coded and designed this website from scratch using skills that I learnt from codecademy.com and w3schools.com because I have never coded a website before. My aim is to create an informative selection of pages for a wide audience because I will explain things simply then go into detail about the physics behind cloud formations and weather systems.

My interest in clouds and the weather is often called upon by friends and family wanting a quick forecast or explanation of a certain phenomenon. Eventually, I would like a career in Meteorology and I am looking into Meteorology (or related) university courses so I can broaden my understanding and learn in depth the processes of our atmosphere and climate. Last year I was lucky enough to get the opportunity to make a weather forecast then present it using green screen technology when I visited the BBC weather tent as part of their BBC Make It Digital tour 2015. Following this, my birthday cake became a cloud classification chart, featuring wispy cirrus clouds through to the almighty cumulonimbus cloud.

I have used a range of reliable sources to compile these pages but avoided plagiarism and ensured everything is in my own words. However, there are some things that are just scientific facts so cannot be reworded completely; these are fairly universally known and used by many websites, books, meteorologists etc.

Thank you.
