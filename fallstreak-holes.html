Fallstreak Holes

Also known as ‘hole punch clouds’, fallstreak holes Form when a patch of supercooled cloud droplets freeze suddenly and sink leaving a visible hole and often a trail of wispy cirrus clouds. Supercooled droplets are droplets of liquid water that remain in this state despite temperatures being well below freezing. Sometimes the remaining ice crystals falling from the cloud refract the sunlight and produce optical phenomena such as circumzenthial arcs.

The cause of the sudden freezing and falling is not yet known, although it can only happen to supercooled water droplets. It is thought that aircraft could have a part to play in their formation because supercooling occurs when there is a lack of freezing nuclei for them to freeze onto. Microscopic particulates from aircraft exhausts are a large source of cloud condensation nuclei (CCN) so they could cause a sudden freezing effect when they fly through some supercooled droplets. It is also thought that the aircraft causes an expansion of air triggering its temperature to drop to -40°C which is cold enough for the droplets to freeze and fall.
